Play Steam Games on Android | Full Guide

Overview

This guide walks you through streaming (and in some cases running) your Steam library on Android handhelds. We’ll cover the easiest path using Steam Link, plus performance tips and common pitfalls.

What You’ll Need

  • Windows/macOS/Linux PC with Steam installed
  • Android handheld/phone with 5GHz Wi-Fi (or USB-C Ethernet)
  • Steam Link app from Google Play

Quick Start

  1. Install Steam Link on your Android device.
  2. Ensure your PC and device are on the same network (preferably 5GHz).
  3. Open Steam Link → pair with your PC → follow the on-screen test.
  4. Launch a game and tweak bitrate/resolution in settings if needed.

Performance Tips

  • Use 5GHz Wi-Fi or a USB-C Ethernet adapter for lowest latency.
  • Cap FPS or reduce resolution if your network is congested.
  • Close background downloads on both PC and Android.

Troubleshooting

  • Black screen: Alt+Tab once on PC; ensure GPU drivers are up to date.
  • Stutter: Lower bitrate, lock to 30/45 FPS, or switch to Ethernet.
  • Controller: In Steam → Settings → Controller, verify layout and glyphs.
